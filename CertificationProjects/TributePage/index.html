Nikola Tesla

The Forgotten Genius Who Electrified the World and Shaped the Future.

Timeline of Tesla's key life events:

  • 1856 - Born in Smiljan, Croatia
  • 1882 - Moved to Paris to work for the Continental Edison Company
  • 1887 - Developed an induction motor that ran on alternating current (AC)
  • 1891 - Invented the Tesla Coil
  • 1893 - Demonstrated the first successful wireless power transmission
  • 1895 - Built one of the first hydroelectric power plants at Niagara Falls
  • 1898 - Demonstrated the first remote-controlled boat
  • 1900 - Began construction of the Wardenclyffe Tower for wireless communication and power transmission
  • 1916 - Received the Edison Medal, the highest honor from the American Institute of Electrical Engineers
  • 1943 - Died in New York City

Tesla’s most important contributions:

  • Alternating Current (AC) System - Tesla’s AC power system became the standard for electricity distribution, changing the world by making long-distance power transmission possible.
  • The Tesla Coil - An invention that contributed to the development of radio technology and wireless power transmission.
  • Wireless Communication - Tesla conducted pioneering experiments that laid the foundation for radio, remote control, and wireless communication technologies.
  • Induction Motor - Tesla’s induction motor, powered by AC, revolutionized industrial power, making electric motors more efficient and widespread.
  • Hydroelectric Power at Niagara Falls - Tesla’s work in the development of the first large-scale hydroelectric power plant at Niagara Falls marked a milestone in renewable energy production.
  • Wardenclyffe Tower - Though never completed, Tesla’s Wardenclyffe Tower was a bold vision for worldwide wireless communication and energy transmission.
  • Remote Control - Tesla demonstrated the first remote-controlled boat, showcasing his ability to transmit power and data wirelessly.
